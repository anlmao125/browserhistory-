A history of browsers

Introduction

A web browser is a software application that allows users to access and view information.

The first web browser was created in 1990 by Tim Berners-Lee, the inventor of the WorldWideWeb.

Since then, many browsers have been developed, each with its own features and capabilities.

In this document, we will explore the history of some of the most popular web browsers.

Internet Explorer

Internet Explorer was launched by Microsoft in 1995 as part of the Windows 95 operating system.

It quickly rose to dominance in the early 2000s, capturing over 90% of the global browser market due to its integration with Windows.

However, it became notorious for security vulnerabilities, slow performance, and lack of support for modern web standards.

As competitors emerged, particularly Firefox and later Chrome, Internet Explorer’s user base dwindled. Microsoft officially retired the browser in 2022, replacing it with Microsoft Edge.

Firefox

Firefox was released in 2004 by the Mozilla Foundation as a streamlined, open-source alternative to the bloated Mozilla Suite.

Initially named "Phoenix," it gained popularity for its speed, customizability, and commitment to privacy and web standards.

Firefox played a major role in breaking Internet Explorer's monopoly but eventually lost significant market share to Google Chrome.

Despite the competition, Firefox remains a respected browser among users who prioritize open-source software and data privacy.

Chrome

Chrome was introduced by Google in 2008, revolutionizing the browser market with its speed, minimalist design, and advanced JavaScript engine.

Built on the Chromium open-source project, Chrome rapidly overtook competitors and became the world’s most used browser by 2012.

Its success spurred the development of other Chromium-based browsers . While Chrome is praised for performance and innovation, it has also faced criticism over privacy concerns.

It continues to dominate the browser landscape today.
